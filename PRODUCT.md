# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Cyber-fashion, tactical, and tech-wear enthusiasts who value futuristic aesthetics, industrial design, and highly curated tech-subculture apparel. They are extremely design-literate, seeking a digital experience that feels like an exclusive technical design label rather than a generic retail storefront.

## Product Purpose
To serve as a high-aesthetic, immersive digital storefront (STITCH DSTRKT) for curated tech-wear fashion. The platform sources live items via AliExpress dropshipping but elevates them under a premium, highly stylized editorial presentation. Success is defined by user immersion, brand trust, and a seamless, high-performance checkout flow.

## Brand Personality
Tactical, industrial, sophisticated, and precise.

## Anti-references
- **The SaaS-Cream Template**: Avoid saturated "SaaS-cream" gradients, soft-corner rounded cards, playful illustrations, and generic tech-start-up typography.
- **Mainstream Consumer Retail**: Avoid flat, generic Shopify-like white-background grids, aggressive sales banners, generic discount badges, and playful retail copy.
- **Clunky Cyber-Gamer Tropes**: Avoid over-the-top, saturated "cyberpunk" clichés (neon pinks/greens, heavy scan lines, glowing text shadows, cheap hacking terminal fonts) that feel like toys rather than professional high-end editorial fashion.

## Design Principles
- **Editorial Lookbook Presentation**: Present products with the gravity of a high-end physical lookbook. Grid layouts should have varied rhythm, ample breathing room, and bold structural framing.
- **Tactical Utility & Precision**: Interactive components, borders, and animations should feel like a high-performance blueprint or tactical interface—sharp, clean, and satisfyingly mechanical.
- **Sophisticated Monochrome Depth**: Rely on strong typographic hierarchy, structural linework, and subtle micro-textures (like grids and blueprint overlays) for visual interest instead of relying on saturated decorative color.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- Strict contrast compliance (minimum 4.5:1 ratio for all body copy and interactive status labels).
- System-wide sharp contrast boundaries to preserve structural clarity for low-vision users.
- Full respect for user system motion preferences via `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` on all transition-heavy interfaces.
